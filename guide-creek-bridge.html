The Process

How It All Comes Together

1

Mark & Dig the Creek Path

Use a garden hose to lay out a natural S-curve, then dig a 6–8 inch wide, 4–6 inch deep trench with clean angled sides.

2

Line With Landscape Fabric

Press weed barrier fabric into the trench and up the sides, secured with staples. This keeps weeds out and rocks in place for years.

3

Add River Rocks in Layers

Large rocks at the edges for banks, medium pebbles in the center — layered in 3 sizes for a realistic, natural finish.

4

Build the Arched Cedar Bridge

Cut two curved side rails with a jigsaw, space on concrete footings, attach deck boards with galvanized screws, sand and seal.

5

Place, Plant & Enjoy

Set the bridge in place, press footings level, add edge plants like ornamental grass or creeping thyme, and admire your work!
